The World Wants Me to ‘Be Myself’ But Only the Polite Version
The world wants me to ‘be myself’ but only the polite, edited, convenient version.

Everyone loves to say “just be yourself” which is hilarious because the second I do, the hidden animals in people just suddenly wake up and start gnawing at my sanity. Like, are we being for real? Yeah, sure. I’ll be myself but don’t blame me when I lose my shit.

The rules of ‘be yourself’ no-one admits:
• Be confident but not that confident.
• Have opinions but only the ones we agree with.
• Speak your mind unless it makes someone uncomfortable.
• Be unique but don’t stand out too much.
• Be honest but only if the truth is cute and convenient.

I mean, seriously? We must filter every thought like we’re running our own PR for our personalities. Do we seriously have to filter ourselves to fit in? If being ‘myself’ is too much, that sounds like a ‘them’ problem. If I have to sand down every edge just to make people comfortable, what’s the point of having a personality? Honestly? I’d rather be different than ‘original.’ Wouldn’t you?

The chaos always begins the same way: one dog, somewhere in the distance, lets out a single bark that somehow sounds like someone has stubbed their toe.

Within seconds, every single dog in the village has a turn, creating a racket that can only be described as: “Someone listening to their phone on full blast.”

No one knows what throws them off. Some assumed triggers by families vary of:
• A gust of wind that suspiciously sounds like a whistle.
• A leaf flying by with a look of disgust.
• Or even the insane concept of being awake before 8AM.

Some residents suggest the canines may be assembling their choir with military precision.
I’m convinced they hold meetings through Skype when we are away.

The barking usually reaches maximum volume at the exact moment you were finally about to sit down and relax after a long day at work.

This leaves locals feeling ready to sue the entire canine population.

Despite the daily chaos, many admit the barking has become part of West Hallam life.
It’s consistent.
It’s loud.
And it absolutely doesn’t care that you have college in an hour.

Well, at least someone in this village has some variation of entertainment. Can’t blame the dogs, at least they’re talkative.

Everyone Wants a Voice – No One Wants to Listen
Everyone wants a voice – no one wants to listen.

Voices are everywhere, but real attention? Extinct.

There’s this thing I keep noticing, and honestly it pisses me off: Everyone wants to talk, but no one wants to hear a damn thing. I mean, I see videos of people singing original songs and the lyrics are fucking deep, mate. Like actual gut-wrenching songs but no one concentrates on the lyrics, it’s the voice or face card that matters. Sure, why not let’s concentrate on rating their looks instead of concentrating on how flaming depressing the lyrics are. As a society we walk through life with our eyes closed and whenever someone tries to speak up, no one listens long enough to change the world.

It feels like the whole world is stuck on broadcast mode, like – everyone’s shouting into the void hoping someone claps. You have to be heard to make a change and if no one hears you and understands the first time? Then, fucking hell, don’t give up. Sounds flaming cliché, doesn’t it? But we all scroll on TikTok, Insta, Snap or wherever and all we hear is noise. People have thousands or millions of views, but they use them on stupid pranks or makeup tutorials. What about our main global issues? Why is it that everyone who gives a fuck about our lives haven’t got the audience for our voices.

Someone needs to listen because it gets really annoying when someone speaks but they aren’t understood.

Conversations now feel like – Who can talk the fastest, the loudest, the most dramatic? The funny part? The loudest ones usually have nothing important to say.
In this world? It’s always “listen to ME,” never – “Hey, what did YOU mean by that?” It’s frustrating, being listened to but not heard. People would rather watch a shitty video with stupid cats in than listen to the concerns of others. Are we being for real? The cats don’t have a point to say, we do, so listen up.

I realised something recently – listening is basically a superpower now. Nobody does it. The world is loud enough already, so don’t try to be the loudest, try to be the one who gets it.

And honestly? At this point, I’m convinced most people don’t even want conversations — they want background noise that agrees with them. They want voices they can mute whenever it gets too real. They want “awareness” as an aesthetic, not a responsibility.

Because actually listening?
Actually giving a shit?
Actually letting someone’s words hit you hard enough to change something?
That’s too much effort for a world addicted to shortcuts.

So yeah, keep scrolling. Keep drowning in the noise you pretend you hate. Keep ignoring the people who actually have something to say. One day, when everything finally collapses and you’re looking around for answers, you’ll realise the truth:

The voices that could’ve saved you were the ones you refused to hear.

But don’t worry.
By then?
It’ll be far too late — and at least the cats will still be entertaining you.

Breaking Barriers: Milestones in the Women's Rights Movement
Let’s talk about rights. The battle women have fought day in and day out for centuries just to survive. The change from the 1700s to the 21st century. Has it changed drastically? Do you know all the history? Are we doing enough?

1975. Nineteen. Seventy. Five. Women finally obtained the right to equal pay. This meant women could legally earn the same as men for doing the same amount of work. It was a crucial step towards equality in the world and workplace. Even today, it reminds us all how long women had to wait for this simple, human right. Women got the right to equal pay when the Equal Pay Act 1970 came into force on 29th December 1975. This legislation was a major step in ensuring that men and women receive equal pay for equal work, marking a pivotal moment in the fight for gender equality.
In the late 19th to early 20th century, women gained the right to freedom. Personal freedom such as universities. This meant women could legally choose her own path in life and ensure her own future. It was a weighty decision for women who wanted more for their lives, which was most, and this choice meant women could become lawyers, officers of the law or even prime ministers.

For example, Margaret Thatcher became the first female Prime Minister of the UK in 1979. Thatcher was seen as a divisive figure, but she proved women can do the same jobs men can, if not better.

Women’s rights have changed over the years. From being their husband’s property to becoming lawyers and Prime Ministers. Gaining the right to vote, the right to owning their own property, and equal pay. Each step has been crucial progress to the world. These changes remind us how change is created one step at a time.

The myth of the perfect household kid.

Everyone loves the idea of a “perfect kid.”
The one who magically knows what needs doing.
The one who senses chores telepathically.
The one who predicts moods before they even shift.

Then something goes wrong. When the dishwasher isn’t emptied, the towel isn’t on the hook, or when something wasn’t cleaned because NO ONE SAID A WORD? Suddenly then its our fault. Not because we ignored instructions, or refused to help. But because no one actually told us it needed doing in the first place.
The “perfect household kid” doesn’t exist.
It’s a flaming myth.
If something is wrong, we’re the default target.
If something is forgotten, it’s our responsibility – even if no one told us.

Then there’s the guilt.
Not the loud kind. The quiet kind.
The “why didn’t you just know?” kind.
The “I shouldn’t have to ask” kind.

Except… they should ask.
That’s how communication works.
That’s how respect works.
That’s how families work — or how they’re supposed to.

We’re not lazy for missing something we weren’t told.
We’re not bad for not living up to some invisible checklist.
We’re not broken for feeling exhausted by the constant guessing game.
We’re human.
And humans aren’t mind-readers.

The only “perfect kid” is the one in their imagination — not the one standing there trying their best.

4. “You’ve not even grown up yet, stop stressing.” – Yeah, sure. How am I supposed to chill when all I see on the news is chaos?

About

Orbis Vox is a space for the thoughts that don’t fit neatly into conversations, classrooms, or Instagram captions. It exists somewhere between being told to “just cope” and realising that pretending everything is fine is genuinely exhausting.

This site isn’t here to be motivational or comforting. It doesn’t offer five steps to fix your life or pretend growing up is simple if you have the right mindset. It’s about the messier reality — being young, overwhelmed, observant, and painfully aware that a lot of systems, expectations, and social norms don’t actually work for everyone.

The writing here focuses on exhaustion, pressure, family dynamics, education, identity, and the quiet survival mode a lot of people slip into without realising. Especially young people. Especially the ones who look like they’re managing just fine.

Blake is a college student and the person behind Orbis Vox. She writes honestly, sometimes bluntly, because softening everything to make it easier to digest usually means lying. She isn’t trying to inspire anyone, fix anyone, or make difficult topics feel comfortable. That’s not the point.

If you’re reading this and feel seen, slightly uncomfortable, or mildly attacked — that’s intentional. Orbis Vox isn’t here to reassure you that everything is okay. It’s here to say that if things aren’t okay, you’re not strange, broken, or alone for noticing.
