Discrimination shown as trans girls are banned from Girl Guide groups
From 2nd December 2025, trans girls are banned from joining Girl Guide groups within the UK. Why was this decision made, and do you agree with it?

This new decision means that girls who were assigned male at birth will no longer be able join Girl Guide groups. The trustees within the organisation that oversees these groups says, “Trans girls and young women and others not recorded female at birth, will no longer be able to join Girlguiding as new young members.” They also said that there would be no changes for young women who are already members but more information on this topic will be shared next week. – BBC News.
Many people from the public think it is unfair and discriminative against trans women. TransActual said: “There is no problem being solved here, only harm done.” My opinion, this ban seems unfair because every girl should have the right to join. This decision excludes girls who only want to belong by being who they are. – BBC News.

What do you think – should all girls be allowed to join, or do you agree with the rules that are set in place?
Students Organize Nationwide School Strike
Thousands of students across the country have organized a coordinated school strike to demand more action on climate change. The movement, led by youth activists, is gaining momentum, with protests held outside government buildings and in city centers. Students expressed frustration over slow policy changes and urge lawmakers to take immediate measures to combat environmental issues.
"We need to be heard, and we are taking our future into our own hands," said one student leader. Teachers and parents have shown mixed reactions, ranging from support to concern over missed lessons. The strike highlights the growing influence of young voices in shaping national policy and awareness on critical issues affecting future generations.

West Hallam’s Dog Choir Wakes Residents
Residents across West Hallam were startled awake at an insane hour this morning as the village’s infamous “Dog Choir” began another full-volume performance.

The chaos always begins the same way: one dog, somewhere in the distance, lets out a single bark that somehow sounds like someone has stubbed their toe.

Within seconds, every single dog in the village has a turn, creating a racket that can only be described as: “Someone listening to their phone on full blast.”

No one knows what throws them off. Some assumed triggers by families vary of:
• A gust of wind that suspiciously sounds like a whistle.
• A leaf flying by with a look of disgust.
• Or even the insane concept of being awake before 8AM.

Some residents suggest the canines may be assembling their choir with military precision.
I’m convinced they hold meetings through Skype when we are away.

The barking usually reaches maximum volume at the exact moment you were finally about to sit down and relax after a long day at work.

This leaves locals feeling ready to sue the entire canine population.

Despite the daily chaos, many admit the barking has become part of West Hallam life.
It’s consistent.
It’s loud.
And it absolutely doesn’t care that you have college in an hour.

Well, at least someone in this village has some variation of entertainment. Can’t blame the dogs, at least they’re talkative.

Breaking Barriers: Milestones in the Women's Rights Movement
Let’s talk about rights. The battle women have fought day in and day out for centuries just to survive. The change from the 1700s to the 21st century. Has it changed drastically? Do you know all the history? Are we doing enough?

1975. Nineteen. Seventy. Five. Women finally obtained the right to equal pay. This meant women could legally earn the same as men for doing the same amount of work. It was a crucial step towards equality in the world and workplace. Even today, it reminds us all how long women had to wait for this simple, human right. Women got the right to equal pay when the Equal Pay Act 1970 came into force on 29th December 1975. This legislation was a major step in ensuring that men and women receive equal pay for equal work, marking a pivotal moment in the fight for gender equality.
In the late 19th to early 20th century, women gained the right to freedom. Personal freedom such as universities. This meant women could legally choose her own path in life and ensure her own future. It was a weighty decision for women who wanted more for their lives, which was most, and this choice meant women could become lawyers, officers of the law or even prime ministers.

For example, Margaret Thatcher became the first female Prime Minister of the UK in 1979. Thatcher was seen as a divisive figure, but she proved women can do the same jobs men can, if not better.

Women’s rights have been a long road, and there’s still work to do. But it’s inspiring to see progress and to know that every effort, no matter how small, adds to the larger movement.

Teenage Exhaustion
Being a teenager in 2025 is exhausting. Between school, social media, homework, and trying to figure out who you are, it can feel impossible to catch a break.

My days start early and end late. There’s constant pressure to succeed academically, socially, and personally. I often feel like I’m running a race with no finish line.
Sleep becomes a luxury, and self-care feels like a distant dream. Yet, despite the fatigue, there’s also a sense of excitement and potential. Each day is a challenge, but also an opportunity to grow, learn, and shape the person I’m becoming.

Finding balance is hard, but even small victories—finishing an assignment, making someone laugh, or taking a moment for yourself—can feel monumental in the teenage world.
